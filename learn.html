Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common insurance questions we hear.

How much insurance do I really need?

The amount of insurance you need depends on your assets, income, dependents, and risk tolerance. A general rule is to have enough coverage to replace your income and protect your assets, but a licensed agent can help determine the exact amount based on your specific situation.

Will shopping for quotes hurt my credit score?

Most insurance companies perform "soft" credit checks that don't affect your credit score when providing quotes. Only hard credit checks impact your score, and these are typically done only after you've decided to purchase a policy.

Can I change my insurance anytime?

Yes, you can generally cancel or change your insurance policy at any time. However, timing matters for avoiding gaps in coverage and potential penalties. It's best to have your new policy in place before canceling your current one.

What's the difference between an agent and a broker?

An agent typically represents one or a few insurance companies, while a broker works independently and can shop multiple companies for you. Both are licensed professionals, but brokers generally have access to more options.
